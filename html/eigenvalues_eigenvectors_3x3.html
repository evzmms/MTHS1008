3 x 3 Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

1.13. 3 x 3 Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors#

Eigenvalue problems for \(3\times3\) systems are of the same form as we have met already.

For a \(3\times3\) matrix \({\bf{A}}\), we need to find eigenvalues \(\lambda\) and eigenvectors \(\boldsymbol{x}\) such that

\[\begin{equation*} {\bf{A}}\boldsymbol{x} = \lambda\boldsymbol{x}. \end{equation*}\]

In the case of a \(3\times3\) system the characteristic polynomial will be a cubic and, in general, will have three roots and a corresponding number of eigenvectors.

Example 1.25

Find the eigenvalues and corresponding eigenvectors of the matrix

\[\begin{equation*} {\bf{A}} = \left(\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 \end{array}\right) \end{equation*}\]

Solution

  • Step 1
    Find the characteristic polynomial

\[\begin{eqnarray*} \textrm{det}\left({\bf{A}} - \lambda{\bf{I}}\right) &=& \left|\begin{array}{ccc} 1 - \lambda & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 - \lambda & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 - \lambda \end{array}\right| \\[2ex] &=& \left|\begin{array}{cc} 2 & 1~ \\ 1 - \lambda & 2~ \end{array}\right| - \left(3 + \lambda\right) \left|\begin{array}{cc}1 - \lambda & 2 \\0 & 1 - \lambda \end{array}\right|\quad\begin{array}{l}\textrm{Expanding by} \\ \textrm{the }3^{\textrm{rd}}\textrm{ row}\end{array}\\[1ex] &=&\left[4 - \left(1 - \lambda\right)\right] - \left(3 + \lambda\right)\left[\left(1 - \lambda\right)^2\right]\\ &=&\left(3 + \lambda\right) - \left(3 + \lambda\right)\left[\left(1 - \lambda\right)^2\right]\\ &=&\left(3 + \lambda\right)\left[1 - \left(1 - \lambda\right)^2\right]\\ &=&\left(3 + \lambda\right)\left[2\lambda - \lambda^2\right]\\ &=&\left(3 + \lambda\right)\lambda\left(2 - \lambda\right). \end{eqnarray*}\]

  • Step 2
    Solve the characteristic equation
    \(\displaystyle \qquad\qquad\qquad \textrm{det}\left({\bf{A}} - \lambda{\bf{I}}\right) = \left(3 + \lambda\right)\lambda\left(2 - \lambda\right) = 0.\)
    The solutions are

\[\begin{equation*} \lambda = -3, \quad \lambda = 0, \quad \lambda = 2. \end{equation*}\]

  • Step 3. For each eigenvalue, find the corresponding eigenvector

    • Case \(\lambda = -3\):
      Write out
      \(\displaystyle \qquad\qquad\qquad \left({\bf{A}} - \lambda{\bf{I}}\right)\boldsymbol{x} = {\bf{0}} \)
      in full to give

    \[\begin{eqnarray*} &\left[\left(\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 \end{array}\right) + 3\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\right]\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad \left(\begin{array}{ccc} 4 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 4 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\ \left\{ \begin{array}{rcl} 4x_2 + 2x_3 &=& 0 \\ x_1 &=& 0\end{array} \!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\right. \end{eqnarray*}\]

       If these last two conditions are met we automatically have \(4x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3=0\).

       Hence any vector of the form \(\displaystyle r\left(\begin{array}{r} 0 \\ 1 \\ -2\end{array}\right)\) is an eigenvector.

       (where \(r\ne0\) is an arbitrary constant.)

    • Case \(\lambda = 0\):
      Write out

    \[\begin{equation*} \left({\bf{A}} - \lambda{\bf{I}}\right)\boldsymbol{x} = {\bf{0}} \end{equation*}\]
    \[\begin{eqnarray*} &\left[\left(\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 \end{array}\right) + 0\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\right]\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad \left(\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad \left\{ \begin{array}{rcl} x_2 + 2x_3 &=& 0 \\ x_1 - 3x_3 &=& 0\end{array} \!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\right. \end{eqnarray*}\]

       If these last two conditions are met we automatically have \(x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3=0\).

       Hence any vector of the form \(\displaystyle s\left(\begin{array}{r} 3 \\ -2 \\ 1\end{array}\right)\) is an eigenvector.

       (where \(s\ne0\) is an arbitrary constant.)

    • Case \(\lambda = 2\):
      Write out

    \[\begin{equation*} \left({\bf{A}} - \lambda{\bf{I}}\right)\boldsymbol{x} = {\bf{0}} \end{equation*}\]
    \[\begin{eqnarray*} &\left[\left(\begin{array}{rrr} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -3 \end{array}\right) - 2\left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{array}\right)\right]\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad \left(\begin{array}{rrr} -1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & -5 \end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{array}\right) &=&\ \left(\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right)\\[2ex] \Rightarrow & \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\ \left\{ \begin{array}{rcl} -x_2 + 2x_3 &=& 0 \\ x_1 - 5x_3 &=& 0\end{array} \!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\!\right. \end{eqnarray*}\]

       If these last two conditions are met we automatically have \(-x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3=0\).

       Hence any vector of the form \(\displaystyle t\left(\begin{array}{r} 5 \\ 2 \\ 1\end{array}\right)\) is an eigenvector.

       (where \(t\ne0\) is an arbitrary constant.)
